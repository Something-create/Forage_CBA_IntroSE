Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

Ensuring strong cybersecurity practices is crucial to protect your personal and financial information from cyber threats. Here are some key practices to follow:

  • Maintain Device Security:
    • Install a reliable antivirus application to help defend against malware and viruses. Also, get a VPN to help encrypting your data and mask your IP address.
    • Regularly update your devices including you operating systems and financial applications to patch any security vulnerabilities.
  • Enable Multi-factor Authentication (MFA):
    • Use MFA wherever possible to add an extra layer of security beyond passwords, such as a code sent to your phone. Also, use a Biometric authentication with your standard passwords.
    • All passwords should be changes every 3 months.
  • Avoid Opening Suspicious Emails:
    • Do not click on links or download attachments from unfamiliar or suspicious emails to prevent malware infections and phishing attempts.
    • If you receive a suspicious email or message, be sure to report.
  • Avoid Using Public Wi-Fi:
    • Do not conduct financial transactions or accessing sensitive information over public Wi-Fi networks. Use a VPN for a secure connection if necessary.
  • Monitor Financial Accounts Regularly:
    • Review bank and credit card statements frequently for unauthorized transactions or unusual activity.
    • Report any suspicious activity or potential scams to your financial institution promptly.
    • Do not give any personal information to a not secure website. When shopping online, stick to trusted websites and do not give your debit card information. Some alternative ways of payments include PayPal or google pay.
If you have been scammed or your bank account has been hacked. Then you should immediately freeze your account and report them immediately. Make sure to change all your passwords and usernames of your accounts.
Implementing these practices will help your Cyber Hygiene. This will reduce the risk of being a victim to a cybercrime. Always, stay vigilant and proactive in protecting your financial information to safeguard your financial well-being.
